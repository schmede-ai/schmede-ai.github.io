On this Document
Wrath is a contemporary grimdark universe, grounded in Catholic cosmology and told primarily through short-form visual work.

This volume holds the working canon of the world — the rules of its sky, the ordering of its choirs, the ecosystem of its enemies, the vocabulary its inhabitants use. It is not a treatment, not a marketing deck, not a screenplay. It is a reference object, written so that every short built inside this universe inherits a world already ruled.

The universe has one claim it will not release: that the sacred is treated with weight. No winking, no irony, no self-awareness. The register is heavy by design. Readers looking for the tone should expect Old Testament judgment delivered through modern firearms, and a mercy that is never sentimental.

The World
Wards that held for generations are failing. Civil authority is in visible collapse. The Emergency Alert System is still functional. The language of the bulletin has not yet learned the word for what is happening.
I · The World01
§ 01  ·  The Breach
The Collapse is Ongoing
Not a single event. A phase.

The world has been degrading for a long time. Wards dissolve at the rate faith dissolves. The institutions built to hold the line — sacramental volume, consecrated ground, the ordinary operation of the Church — have decayed past the threshold that kept Hell from physically manifesting.

What the audience sees is the phase where the degradation became visible and unrecoverable.

Prayers go up fast. Answers come down slow.

Rank is not measured by power but by proximity. The lower orders are operational and seen often — they move through the contested sky and make Heaven’s work visible on Earth.

The higher orders are rare and decisive. When one appears, it is because geometry has permitted it. The register of the scene changes with the arrival.

II · Heaven05
Seraphim

Six wings, per Isaiah 6 — two of them cover the face. Monumental, composed, still. They descend when Heaven answers, and the register of the scene changes on contact.

Ophanim

Concentric gold wheels full of eyes, per Ezekiel 1. The Thrones’ manifestation on Earth. They operate above the contested sky, in the geometry where opposition cannot reach. When one appears, the outcome is already decided.

The Powers
Exousiai  ·  the order tasked with preventing the assaults of demons

In Ephesians 6:12, Paul names the exousiai — the Powers — as the heavenly counterpart to the demonic in the contested lower heavens. Aquinas assigns them a specific office: the choir whose duty is to prevent the assaults of demons.

In the present volume, four of them walk the Earth, each bearing a classical Latin virtue as her name and her function. They wear medieval plate. They carry modern firearms. They can be wounded. They can be killed. They fail. They are what arrives when a prayer gets through.

II · Heaven08
Power I  ·  Old Testament
Iustitia
Justice

Rendering due. The ordering virtue. Right proportion.

Leader of the walking roster. In classical reading, justice is the ordering virtue — the one that arranges all others in their right proportion. When Iustitia arrives, she has already decided what is due. The target is not yet aware that the decision has been made.

Mercy is offered first. Sentence follows refusal. This is the structural rule that makes her Old Testament judgment read as exhausted mercy rather than arbitrary cruelty.

Weapon: Deus Vult — “God wills it.”  ·  Consecrated precision long-arm.
II · Heaven09
Power II  ·  Old Testament
Zelus
Holy Zeal

Holy wrath. Ira per zelum. Passionate opposition to evil.

Modern English collapses her into "fanaticism" and reads the valence as negative. Classical Latin treats Zelus as correctly-ordered fire: the virtue whose opposite is not calmness but indifference to evil. She is staged as righteous fire, not recklessness.

Where Iustitia deliberates, Zelus burns. Volume of force is her register. A suburb on fire is her establishing shot.

Weapon: Consumet Eos — “It shall consume them.”  ·  Consecrated shotgun, high-volume carbine.
II · Heaven10
Power III  ·  Old Testament
Vindicta
Righteous Requital

An aspect of justice. Lex talionis. Return of injury in kind.

Modern audiences read vindicta as forbidden vengeance — personal, corrupted, dark. Classical Latin reads it as an aspect of justice: the measured return of what was given. If Iustitia determines what is due, Vindicta is the register in which it is repaid with a symmetry the recipient cannot miss.

The close-quarters Power. Precise. Personal. The mirror-return of wrong.

Weapon: Mihi Vindicta — “Vengeance is mine.”  ·  Consecrated sidearm.
II · Heaven11
Power IV  ·  New Testament
Misericordia
Mercy

Heart-with-the-suffering. Response to pain, regardless of guilt.

The single New Testament Power on the walking roster. She is alone on her side by design — the three-to-one ratio lets her bear the structural weight of grace without softening the register of the whole. When she arrives, the short declares itself. It is not the one where a cultist is executed. It is the one where a civilian is carried out.

Not sentimental. Mercy in this universe is a discipline.

Weapon: Dona Requiem — “Grant rest.”  ·  Consecrated rifle & inscribed sidearm.
II · Heaven12
§ 06  ·  Above the Powers
Dominions
The command layer between Archangels and Thrones. They cast their shadow over the battlefields they order, and are not ordinarily seen by the civilians they manage. Volume I treats them at the edge of frame; later volumes may bring them in.
II · Heaven13
§ 07  ·  The Third Sphere
Guardians

Common angels. Unarmed. Oriented toward individual human lives. A canonical open question stands over them: they may be the order that has been recalled or is no longer reliably dispatched — which would explain why the Powers are the de facto response tier in the present era.

II · Heaven14
§ 08  ·  The Class of Blessed Arms
Arma Consecrata
Blessed & inscribed. Categorically more effective than what is not.
In the Field
Consumet Eos
Zelus sighting down her consecrated shotgun.

Any firearm can be consecrated. Each Power carries the version appropriate to her office. A blessed weapon wielded by a walking Power is peak effectiveness. A blessed weapon wielded by a faithful human is strong. A field-blessed conventional weapon is still effective. An unblessed weapon works, but degraded. A demonic attempt to wield a consecrated weapon fails or backfires.

Personal Inscriptions  ·  per Power
Deus Vult “God wills it.” Iustitia
Consumet Eos “It shall consume them.” Zelus
Mihi Vindicta “Vengeance is mine.” Vindicta
Dona Requiem “Grant rest.” Misericordia

Chaplains can bless ammunition and inscribed weapons in the field. Sacramental action upgrades squad firepower — which is what makes the chaplain load-bearing rather than decorative.

III · Hell17
§ 10  ·  Cosmology
Hell is in the Air
Caelum · Caelum Inferius · Terra.

Daniel 10 — when Daniel prays, the angel sent in response is delayed twenty-one days by opposition in the air above Earth. Paul, in Ephesians, confirms the architecture: principalities, powers, rulers of darkness in high places. Satan is named the prince of the power of the air.

The practical implication: the demonic operates directly adjacent to the human world. Hell’s access is near-instantaneous. Heaven is above and beyond the contested lower heavens. Every response must transit a combat environment.

III · Hell18
§ 11  ·  The Ecosystem
Three Tiers of Opposition
The enemy is organized. It is not uniform.
Commander tier
Fallen Powers

Named antagonists. Inverted-virtue Latin nomenclature (Sacrilega, Crudelitas, Perfidia…) They mirror the walking Powers structurally. The Seven Deadly Sins are off the table as names, by authorial rule.

Human infantry
Cultists

Hooded, armed, modern. Red demonic sigil on tactical gear. Serve the fallen willingly. Human weaknesses — they bleed and fall to conventional fire. The interior of the problem that the EAS cannot quite name on broadcast.

Shock troops
Daemonia

Lesser demons. Red-skinned, horned, cloven-hoofed. Vulgate Latin for the demons Christ casts out — singular daemonium. Pack behavior. Easily killed by a Power. Dangerous to humans in numbers.

· · ·

Princes of Hell — Lucifer, Beelzebub, Asmodeus, Leviathan — exist in the universe but are held in reserve for rare, high-tier use.

III · Hell19
First Named Fallen
Sacrilega
She who defiles what was sacred

The inversion of Pietas. Reverence turned to defiance.

Fallen Powers take their names from the Latin inversion of the virtue they reject. Sacrilega is the first — the warrior’s oath broken. Black feathered wings, horns, black armor, a red sigil on her breastplate. She smokes cigarettes. The detail is kept.

She is a peer to the walking Powers. To kill one like her, an ophanim may have to appear.

Additional slots open within the framework — named as shorts require them.
III · Hell20
Human infantry
Cultists

Burlap hoods, black tactical kit, AK-pattern rifles. The Sigillum Sulphuris — the sulphur sigil — spray-painted on their plate carriers in blood-red. Nobody issued them the mark; they put it on themselves.

Shock troops
Daemonia

Lesser demons — quadrupedal, knee-height, wet-flesh crimson. Reptilian heads, curved horns, claws. A pack, not a vanguard — they flank and swarm rather than hold a line. A Power cuts through them as crowd-clearing. Unaided civilians cannot.
